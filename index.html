The Church History

ACK ST. STEPHENS CHURCH JUJA FARM PARISH

ACK ST. STEPHENS Juja farm is the mother church in ACK Juja farm parish. We take this opportunity to thank the Almighty God for His mercies and protection over this long journey that we celebrate today. The history of this church dates back in the year 1960 when the church was hosted by Juja farm primary school for a number of years. This was until the then directors of juja farm 1976 company allocated the church a plot where it sits today. Since inception, the church has served under various parishes as listed below:
1. Thika parish
2. Kiamwangi parish
3. Juja parish
4. Thiririka parish
5. Juja farm parish
Through the commitment and resilience of members of the church then, the church organized a number of harambees to put up a permanent church. Though the church had very few members, many gave in kind and others labored to ensure that the church stands as it is today. Under the stewardship of the then Rev. Wilson Kinuthia, the ground breaking was done in the year 1994 and the permanent building started then. Before this period church was done by iron sheets where congregants worshipped for a number of years. In the year 1997, the then bishop of Mt. Kenya south, the Rt Rev Peter Njenga laid the foundation stone of the church on 2nd March 1997 and named it ACK ST. STEPHENS CHURCH as per the request of the church members. Today, twenty-seven years later we celebrate many blessings from God as a church. The church has been served by various vicars among them.

We take this opportunity to thank the clergy and the pastoral team for their sacrifices to ensure that the church stands firm as it is today.
Due to long distances travelled by some Christians to attend services, ACK ST. Stephens later spread to other areas in the parish and planted other churches with the first. being Karima Simba in 1997, ACK Gikumari (now a parish), ACK Juja house, ACK Mountain view, ACK Mwireri and Salama church (now a parish). Juja farm churches were given a parish status in the year 2000 under the then Rev Beauter Kamau under the name Thiririka Parish. In the year 2002, under the request of the church council, the then Bishop Rt Rev Gideon Githiga changed the name of the parish to ACK Juja Farm Parish The church has carried out various development projects among them
a. Planting of other churches as mentioned above and as the mother church ensuring that they stand on their feet to operate independently
b. Mothers union have managed to buy a plot which they intend to develop to an income earning status. They also have been able to construct a modern kitchen which is used for various church occasions as the one we have today
c. The church now sits on four plots totaling to an acre whereby the extra plots have been purchased by generous contributions of the Christians through various departmental fundraisers.
d. The church has constructed a vicarage which is now rented out and thus an income generating project
e. The church constructed two standard classrooms and bought a van. The school project was run for two years and later suspended to enable the church lay strong foundations and fundamentals before another reopening. The classrooms are currently in use by our teens and Sunday school and for various meetings including at the parish level.
f. The church has greatly improved the praise and worship instruments by modernizing the keyboard, microphones and installation of screens to enhance transmission of services.
During this period, the church has had many church councils lead by various vice chairmen among them the late Elijah Kamau, the Late David Gikuma and currently Moses Kaniaru. It has not been a smooth journey but God has been on our side. We sincerely thank you, Christians in this great church for your continued support of the church through various departments. Your commitment, your tireless giving to the lord, your prayers are not taken for granted. may our lord bless you all and bless ACK St. Stephens church and our entire Juja farm parish. In light of all above, we thank all for your immeasurable support towards these projects which continues to define the image of ACK. St. Stephen Juja farm parish.
